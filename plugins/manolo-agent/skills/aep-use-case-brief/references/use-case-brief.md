# AEP Use-Case Brief

Capture:

1. Business outcome: problem, target behavior, KPI, baseline, target, owner, market, and deadline.
2. Experience: audience, trigger, channels or consumers, frequency, exclusions, and fallback behavior.
3. Data: source systems, events and attributes, XDM paths, volume, history, cadence, latency, and quality rules.
4. Identity and Profile: namespaces, primary identity, stitching, Profile enablement, and merge policy.
5. Audience: eligibility, lookback, refresh method, suppressions, qualification and exit behavior, expected size.
6. Activation: destination, account, mappings, schedule, file or streaming contract, consent enforcement, and reconciliation.
7. Governance: purpose, legal basis, data minimization, labels, policies, retention, residency, and deletion obligations.
8. Delivery: sandbox path, dependencies, test cases, acceptance criteria, promotion, rollback, monitoring, and hypercare.
9. Decisions: assumptions, unresolved questions, named owners, and due dates.

Before calling the brief implementation-ready, every production-impacting item must be confirmed and testable. A business label such as “known customer” is not sufficient; translate it into explicit data and identity rules.
